Legume

1)Moong Dal:

Place of Taking majority of this crop:
India is the main producer of green grams in the world and almost grown in all the States. It is grown in around 36 lakh hectares of land with the total production of around 17 lakh tonnes of grain with a productivity of around 500 kg/hectare.
Soil:
Moong Dal can be cultivated on a wide range of soil. It gives the best results when grown on well-drained loamy, sandy-loam soils. Waterlogged and saline soils are not suitable for cultivation. Loam to sandy loam soils is considered the best soil for mung bean cultivation.
The best planting season for Moong Dal:
Optimum time for summer moong cultivation is from March to April. Use row spacing of 30 cm and plant to plant spacing of 10 cm for Kharif sowing. For Rabi sowing use row spacing of 22.5 cm and plant to plant spacing of 7 cm. Sow seeds at depth of 4-6 cm.

2)Toor Dal:

Place of Taking majority of this crop:
India has the largest area (3.6 m ha) under pigeonpea, followed by Myanmar (560,000 ha), Kenya (196,000 ha), China (150,000 ha), Malawi (123,000 ha), Uganda (86,000 ha), Mozambique (84,000 ha), Tanzania (68,000 ha) and Nepal (21,000 ha).
Soil:
Soil requirement for Toor Dal Plantation: This crop grows well on all types of soils but loam to sandy loam soil is suitable.
The best planting season for Toor Dal:
Pigeon pea or Toor Dal is a traditionally Kharif crop sown in June-July with the onset of Monsoon in various agro-climatic zones of India. Planting of early pigeon pea or Toor Dal before the onset of monsoon in the month of June is recommended for higher yields.

3)Masoor Dal:

Place of Taking majority of this crop:
Myanmar exports most of it's Masoor dal to India. The top 3 exporters of Masoor dal are India with 8,127 shipments followed by Afghanistan with 170 and Canada at the 3rd spot with 142 shipments.
Soil:
For lentils (masoor) and other pulses, loamy soils, which drain water easily, are required. Lentil does not tolerate flooded or waterlogged soils, and does best on loamy soils. Roots of plants get enough water, air, and space to grow.
The best planting season for Masoor Dal:
Seed should be sown in the mid of October to First week of November. Seeds should be sown in lines 22 cm apart. Under late sowing conditions, row- spacing should be reduced to 20 cm. Sow seeds at depth of 3-4 cm.

4)Black-Eyed Pea:

Place of Taking majority of this crop:
Most cowpeas are grown on the African continent, particularly in Nigeria and Niger.Uttar Pradesh is leading state in production of cowpea in India. Cowpea is a self pollinating crop. Its pods are eaten as vegetable.
Soil:
Chawli can be grown in light, heavy, sandy, loamy soils of almost all types.
The best planting season for Cowpeas/Chawli:
Best time for sowing is from March to Mid-July. while sowing use row to row spacing of 30 cm and plant to plant spacing of 15 cm. sowing depth should be 3-4 cm. Sow seeds with help of pora or seed cum fertilizer drill.

5)Chickpea:

Place of Taking majority of this crop:
Over the last several years, India has been the top producer of chickpeas, also known as garbanzo beans, worldwide. In 2021, the production volume of chickpeas in India amounted to almos 12 million metric tons. Australia came in second at an estimated 876.5 thousand metric tons of chickpeas.
Soil:
Chickpea crops are best suited to well-drained loam and clay loam soils that are neutral to alkaline (pH 6.0 to 9.0) and have good water holding capacity. Sowing will vary between varieties, but can start as early as mid May to the start of July.
The best planting season for Chickpea:
Chickpeas need at least 3 months of cool, but frost-free, days to mature. In the tropics, garbanzos are grown in the winter and in cooler, temperate climes, they are grown between spring to late summer.
